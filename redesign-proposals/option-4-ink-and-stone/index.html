March 5, 2026 · Draft

Some ideas are like river stones — shaped not by intent but by constant gentle pressure. You carry them in your pocket, turning them over, and one day they feel smooth enough to share.

The crack in the bowl is not a flaw. It is the story the bowl tells. — Kintsugi proverb

I keep coming back to this idea that imperfection is not failure. It's proof of living. A perfectly written sentence has nowhere to go. A rough one invites you to sit with it, to turn it over, to polish it with your own understanding.

Maybe that's why journals work. They're not meant to be perfect. They're meant to be true.

Design Rationale

Color Philosophy

Warm dark palette inspired by charcoal, stone, and aged wood. Gold (#c9a84c) accent borrowed from current site but used more sparingly. Rust and sage as secondary tones.

Typography

Noto Serif JP for headings — Japanese typographic tradition. IBM Plex Sans at weight 200-300 for body — engineering precision meets lightness. Kanji characters as decorative nav icons.

Layout Shift

Asymmetric balance: entries stagger left-right. Generous padding creates zen-like whitespace. Vertical text accent in hero. Grid-less "now" section uses simple 2-column borders.

Editor Changes

Maximum whitespace in writing area. Left-border active state on nav (like a scroll marker). Minimal toolbar with kanji-inspired icons. Date line below title. Block quote styled as indented with left border.

Key Departure

Strips away ornamentation entirely. No glow effects, no animations. Beauty in restraint. Every element earns its space. Haiku dividers between sections.

Wabi-sabi Principle

Embraces imperfection. Cards don't have hover animations — the content speaks. Ink-wash textures (subtle radial gradients) suggest brushwork. Overall feel: a stone monastery's reading room.
