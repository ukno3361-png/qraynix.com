New Entry

When photons bend through glass, they carry stories. Each wavelength a different memory, splitting into spectrums of understanding.

I've been thinking about transparency in design — not the kind that means invisible, but the kind that means honest. When you layer frosted glass over color, you get something neither fully revealed nor hidden.

That's what good writing does too. It doesn't lay everything bare. It gives you enough light to see by, and trusts you to find the rest.

Design Rationale

Color Philosophy

Three-color aurora gradient: Pink (#ff6ec7) → Purple (#7b68ee) → Cyan (#00d4ff). Used sparingly on text, borders, and ambient backgrounds. Base is near-black with blue undertone.

Typography

Playfair Display (serif) for headings — elegant, editorial. Inter at weight 200-300 for body — ultra-light, airy. Generous letter-spacing throughout.

Glass Effect

Every card and panel uses background blur (backdrop-filter: blur 16-24px) with rgba white borders. Creates depth without solid surfaces. Hover reveals gradient top-border.

Editor Changes

Same glassmorphism in the editor. Rounded pill-shaped inputs and nav items. Gradient publish button. Highlighted words use gradient text effect instead of bold/color.

Key Departure

Replaces solid dark surfaces with translucent glass. Runes replaced with flowing aurora gradients. Overall feel shifts from "ancient stone" to "frozen light." More modern, less mystical.

Animation

Slow-drifting aurora blobs (12-15s loops) behind content. Card hover lifts + border glow. No flickering — everything is smooth and breathing. Transitions at 0.25-0.35s.
