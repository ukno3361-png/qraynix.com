There's something about watching seeds push through dark earth that teaches you what no book can. Growth doesn't need sunlight every hour — sometimes it happens in the deep, quiet dark, where roots do their bravest work.
+ ⠿
The garden doesn't care about your timeline. It grows when it's ready. And yet, every morning you show up.
+ ⠿
The practice of patience
+ ⠿
I keep coming back to this idea that patience isn't passive. It's the active decision to keep watering when you can't see results. The soil remembers every drop, even when the surface looks unchanged.
🌱 ⠿
Remember: growth happens in the dark. Don't measure the garden every morning — just keep watering.
